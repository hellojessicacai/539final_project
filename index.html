Coffee Processing Method

Frequently labeled as the most ancient coffee processing technique, natural processing involves allowing the coffee cherry to naturally dry under the sun, resulting in a distinct 'natural' flavor. This approach is best suited for arid climates.

This approach entails immersing the beans in water for approximately a week, a stage commonly referred to as fermentation. Precision in timing is essential to prevent the development of excessively sour or moldy tastes. The fermentation of the beans plays a pivotal role in enhancing the coffee bean's fruity and acidic flavors.

This method combines elements of both natural and wet processing. After removing the outer layer of the coffee cherry, the beans undergo immediate washing and drying to prevent bacterial growth from the mucilage sugars. The chosen processing method significantly influences the taste, ranging from sour/fruity to sweet and sugary. The coffee's origin and processing play a crucial role in determining preferred flavor profiles.
